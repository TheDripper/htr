Confronting Climate Change Together

The Haiti Takes Root Coalition is a dynamic coordination structure that facilitates information sharing, promotes learning about best practices and new innovations, and encourages collaboration for more effective activities to strengthen rural livelihoods, protect and renew the environment, manage natural resources and adapt to climate change.

Members of the Haiti Takes Root Coalition contribute to national environment and reforestation goals. While tree planting alone won’t ensure survival in the face of climate change, reforestation and re-greening are critical elements of an integrated response that encompasses environment, economy and energy.

Resilient Productive Landscapes

The RPL project will break the cycle of natural resources degradation and increasing vulnerability among rural families through an integrated approach in four watersheds in the Nippes department. RPL will work at both the farm and community level to simultaneously raise production and productivity using more resilient agriculture practices adapted to agro-ecological zones, which will directly contribute to rebuilding the natural resources base. Support for local production will include inputs like seeds, plants and training as well as support to get products to market, including rural infrastructure.

RPL will also strengthen the institutional enabling environment and build capacity for landscape level governance and management at both national and local levels. Interventions were proposed and vetted through a participatory process that included all local stakeholders and elected officials (read more about the methodology HERE – link to blog post).
